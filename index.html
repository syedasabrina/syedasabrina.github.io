Syeda Sabrina Akter


Hello! I'm Syeda Sabrina Akter, currently in my fourth year of the Computer Science PhD program at George Mason University. My primary research area is in the larger domain of NLP and LLMs Applications in high-stakes specialized domains. The domains I am currentlt focusing on are Educational Assesments and Policy Studies (More Specifically, AI Policies).

In the education domain, I am building AI tools to automate the process of school principals' performance assessment. This involves experiments that look into supervisor score variations and the identification of effective assessment characteristics in education. My approach combines qualitative and quantitative methods, including the training of Large Language Models (LLMs) using zero-shot and few-shot learning techniques, particularly in analyzing principal graphic organizers and understanding various performance metrics.

In the Policy Domain, we are also building systems to analyze and parse large AI policy documents from different countries with and without human involvement, and how we can effectively use LLMs to analyze the documents.

Alongside this, I am equally invested in a project studying Computational Approaches of Multilingual News Framing Analysis. This research leverages LLMs and crowdsourcing to enhance the Framing Analysis Task, examining the complex ways news is framed across different languages and cultures.

I have experience working with Speech Recognition and Translation tasks. I'm lucky to be advised by Prof. Antonis Anastasopoulos and be a part of GMU's NLP lab .

Before starting my PhD at Mason, I worked as a Lecturer in Northern University Bangladesh for two years. I received my Bachelors in Computer Science and Engineering from Bangladesh University of Professionals in 2018. My bachelor's thesis focused on developing a machine learning framework for sorting and classifying unstructured data from resumes.

In my free time I like to read, and travel. One of my bucket list task is to visit all 50 states of the USA within the next few years.

I have already visited 13 states and 1 US territory. (And, DC. Does it count??) (Last updated: Jun 2024)
