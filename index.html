Syeda Sabrina Akter

PhD Candidate | NLP and LLM Evaluation

LLM Evaluation • Human AI Collaboration • Reliable AI Systems in High Stakes Domains

Hi, I am Sabrina, a final year PhD candidate in Computer Science at George Mason University. My research sits at the intersection of NLP, large language models, and evaluation for high stakes expert facing tasks in domains such as educational assessment and AI policy.

I focus on building domain specific evaluation frameworks that measure reliability, bias, and how LLM outputs shape expert judgment in real workflows. My work examines what model quality means when accuracy alone is not sufficient, and how evaluation signals can guide responsible system iteration.

I am advised by Prof. Antonis Anastasopoulos and am part of GMU's NLP Lab. I also have experience in speech recognition and machine translation.

Before starting my PhD, I earned my Bachelor of Science in Computer Science and Engineering from Bangladesh University of Professionals in 2018. My undergraduate thesis focused on building a machine learning framework for sorting and classifying unstructured resume data.

Outside of research, I enjoy reading and traveling. One long term goal is to visit all fifty US states. I have currently visited twenty states and one US territory.
